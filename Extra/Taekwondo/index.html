Taekwondo

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art that dates back over 2,000 years, though it was officially named in 1955. It blends elements from indigenous Korean martial arts like Taekkyon with modern influences. The word Taekwondo is derived from three Korean words: Tae (foot), Kwon (fist), and Do (way or discipline), meaning "the way of the foot and fist."

Taekwondo helps children thrive academically and improves their cognitive and academic performance. Through Taekwondo, children stay physically active, which keeps the brain healthy by boosting cognitive performance, facilitating the growth of new neurons, and improving concentration and memory.

Benefits of taekwondo for children .

  • Physical development
  • Healthy socialization
  • Teaches self-control and discipline
  • Healthy lifestyle fundamentals.
  • Improve Cardiovascular health.
  • Teaches great morals and values.
  • Builds self confidence.

Training Structure

  • Belt System
    • Taekwondo uses a color-coded belt ranking system, starting from white for beginners and progressing to black for mastery. Each belt represents a level of skill and knowledge.
  • Forms (Poomsae)
    • Students practice set patterns of movements called Poomsae, which help develop muscle memory, balance, and understanding of offensive and defensive techniques.
  • Sparring (Kyorugi)
    • Controlled sparring matches help children practice techniques in real-time, learning both offense and defense while maintaining safety.

Safety in Practice

  • Protective Gear
    • During sparring sessions, students wear protective equipment, including helmets, chest protectors, and mouth guards, to minimize the risk of injury.
  • Controlled Environment
    • Sparring and self-defense techniques are practiced in a controlled, supervised environment. Instructors ensure that children are learning safely, with emphasis on proper form and control.
